One-Time Pad!

A free app for you and your allies to exchange secret battle plans.

Learn more »

Heads Up! This app uses all ASCII characters so some ciphertext text may appear blank but it is not. There are just hidden ASCII characters in the text-area.

HOW TO USE IT

    Key Exchange
  1. Generate a random key. You must do this yourself. The key should be at least as long as the message(s) you plan on sending. The longer the better.
  2. In person, exchange the key with your ally (or allies).

    3. Encryption
  3. Type a message (plaintext) that you want to encrypt in the first text-area.
  4. Insert your random key in the second text-area.
  5. Click the encrypt/decrypt button. The encrypted text will appear in the third text-area.
  6. If your message was shorter than your key, some of your key will be unsused. Save the unused portion of your key to encrypt/decrypt subsequent messages.

    7. Message Transfer
  7. Send the encypted message to your ally over any channel (e.g. email).

    8. Decryption
  8. Insert an encrypted message into the first text-area.
  9. Insert your random key in the second text-area.
  10. Click the encrypt/decrypt button. The decrypted text will appear in the third text-area.
  11. If your message was shorter than your key, some of your key will be unsused. Save the unused portion of your key to encrypt/decrypt subsequent messages.

HOW IT WORKS

This one-time pad was built using javascript so that all encryption/decryption is done locally (as opposed to data transferring to and from a server).
